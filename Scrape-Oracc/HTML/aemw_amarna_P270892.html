1Rainey, following Cochavi-Rainey, translates ll. 4-5 as "And as to your welfare, so your welfare, the welfare of your house ...." We understand šulumka in this line to be redundant from the perspective of meaning but written to "correct" the non-normative form šulmuka in the previous line. Therefore we lemmatize the word but omit it from the translation.

1From the photo, the penultimate sign looks to be composed of two Winkelhaken; cf. Moran's note: "šu-x-a: x is neither hi [as in the copy] ... nor ha ...; u over an erasure (or nu, Gordon) is possible." Rainey reads -ha!- with note on p. 1378; cf. CAD Š/3 s.v. šuhû: šu-⸢ha(?)⸣-a. Rainey translates the word as "overlaid" without comment; neither Moran nor the dictionaries offer a translation.

1Cf. AlT 320 and 321 for texts recording the delivery of "stones" of oil.

1Moran restores "[available in your country]" and takes the objects in 27-28 as well as in 29-30 as the objects of uttaššir in l. 30.

1Following Rainey in omitting the determinative NA₄ before habannatu; cf. l. 050.

1Rainey restores kīma arhiš at the beginning of the line, but it seems odd to find this adverbial phrase after the verb it modifies.

1Following Moran, Cochavi-Rainey, Rainey and others in understanding -kunī as a 1 cs dual suffix; cf. CAT I: 81-82, though, where Rainey argues that it is a 2 cs dual suffix.

1a-na-ku-te follows Moran p. 106 n. 10 with hesitation. Rainey reads a-na TUG₂-te, "for garments," but it seems unlikely that the specification of the linen's use would be separated from GADA.MEŠ by so many words.
