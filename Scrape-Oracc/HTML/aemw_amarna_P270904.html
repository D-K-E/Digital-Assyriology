(028) [...] [of] your servant [....] Look, Yanh[amu] (is) with you, so a[sk] him if I did not say to him: "(If) you make an alliance wi[th the son]s of Abdi-Aširta, they will take you." He listened to [me] and protected the cit[ies] of the king, his lord. I spoke in this way to Puwu[ra], but he would not listen [to me] because of Haʾip's words, whose father incited the cities to revolt. You see, Haʾip handed [over] Ṣumur! May the king not remain silent so that he would do such a thing since the commissioner was assassinated. If you remain silent now, then Puhuru will not take a stand in Komidi and all of your city rulers will (likewise) be assassinated.
