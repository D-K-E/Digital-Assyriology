(006) O king, my lord, you are like the sun god (and) like the storm god in the heavens! May the king show concern for his servant. The king, my lord, has appointed me to <gu>ard Tyre, the maidservant of the king, and I (thus) sent a tablet at once to the king, my lord, but he has yet to send back word to me. I am the commissioner of the king, my lord, and I am one who delivers good and bad news just the same to the king, my lord. May the king send twenty footsoldiers to <gu>ard his city so that I may enter in to the king, my lord, and so that I may see his face.

(042) When I hear the name of the king and the name of his army, they will be very afraid and the entire land will be afraid. As for the one who does not follow the king, my lord, the king knows him, because you installed me as the commissioner of Tyre. Zimri-Yidda captured Usu from <his> servant; I (thus) abandoned it. We have no water, no wood, and nowhere to bury the dead. So may the king, my lord, show concern for his servant.

(074) There has been hostility against me [s]ince last year! There is no water and no wood. So may he send a tablet to his servant and may I enter in so that I may see his face. May the king [show concern] for his servant and his city. Let him not a[bandon] his city (and) his land. Why must [the commissioner of] the king, my lord, move [away] from the land? Both [Zimri-Yidda] and the enemy know (that) there is no arm of the king. No[w], a footsoldier [is delivering] my tablet to the king, the sun god, [my] lord. So may the king reply to his servant.
