1Cochavi-Rainey and Rainey translate AŠ G[UN u₃ GU]N mimma as "Among some of the talents," without comment. We follow the dictionaries in seeming ta-ah-ba-ṣi₂ as a nominal form; Cochavi-Rainey and Rainey see it as a 2 m.s. form of habāṣu A, translating "you may rejoice(?)". A damaged sign is visible between 30 and [G]UN in the copy (no photo is available), but it is difficult to identify. Cochavi-Rainey reads 30+[x G]UN, Rainey reads 30 [AŠ G]UN ("30+[1(?) among the tal]ents"). Both Cochavi-Rainey and Rainey omit the following URUDU from their translation. Both Cochavi-Rainey and Rainey read the sign following URUDU as TAR, but from their translation ("multicolored"), presumably they mean TAR₂ (DAR = GUN₃ = barāmu A, "to be multicolored," and its cognates). Both Cochavi-Rainey and Rainey leave the final three signs untranslated.
