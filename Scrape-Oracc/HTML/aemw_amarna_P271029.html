(001) (Speak) [t]o the king, my lord, my sun god, the message of Rib-Haddi, your servant: I fell at the feet of my lord, my sun god, seven times and seven times.

(005) May the king, my lord, heed the words of his loyal servant. Things are grievous for me. The hostility of the sons of Abdi-Aširta is severe. They have entered Amurru. The whole land belong to them. Ṣumur and ʾIrqat (alone) remain to the senior official. I am currently standing pat in Ṣumur because the senior official is distraught due to the hostility. I left Byblos, but Zimri-Yidda [and] Yapaʾ-Haddi are not with me. Now the senior official writes to them repeatedly, but they do not listen to him.

(032) Moreover, may the king, my lord, heed the words of his servant. Send guardsmen to Ṣumur and ʾIrqat in light of the fact that all the guardsmen have run away from Ṣumur. May it be pleasing in the presence of the lord, the sun god of the regions. Give me twenty pairs of horses and send an auxiliary force as quickly as possible to guard Ṣumur. All the remaining guardsmen are distraught; few people are (still) in the city.
