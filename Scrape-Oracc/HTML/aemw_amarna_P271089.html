(023) May the king show concern for his land. The king's land--all of it!--is lost. I am overtaken. There is hostility against me. From the mountains of Seir to Gath-Carmel, there is peace for all the city rulers, but there is only hostility against me! I am treated like an apiru man and I will see the two eyes of the king, my lord, due to the hostility against me. I am positioned like a ship in the heart of the sea!

(034) The strong arm of the king captured Nahrimi and Cush, but now the apiru men are capturing the king's cities. Not even one city ruler remains to the king, my lord. All are lost! You see, as for Turbaṣu, he was killed at the gate Sillu; the king remained silent. (And) you see, as for Zimri-Yidda of Lachish, servants allied with the apiru men struck him (to death)! (Then there is) Yaptih-Haddi, (also) killed [at] the gate of Sillo; [the king] remained silent. [Why] has [the king] not called th[em] to reckoning?
