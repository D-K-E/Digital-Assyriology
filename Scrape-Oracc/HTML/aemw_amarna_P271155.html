1awāssu is omitted from Rainey's translation

1There are three different possibilities for the word glossed here and written as u₂-pu-ti in 151:20. There are three potential options, as I see it: 1) an Egyptian loan, wpwt "mission," suggested by Ranke (1910:26) and followed by Albright (1937:196 nn. 4-5), as well as Rainey only for the example in EA 152; 2) a NWS loan, ʿbd "service, suggested by Grave (1982:166 n. 30) and followed by Moran (1992:239 n.1) and Liverani (1998:157 n. 42); and 3) a NWS loan, yupi "beauty," suggested by Rainey, but applied only in EA 151. First of all, whatever is happening, it seems untenable to posit two different words, as Rainey does. While the Egyptian loan works linguistically, the semantics is an entirely different matter. NWS ʿbd is intriguing, but I see two hurdles here: one is the unlikelihood of both a labial and dental shift and another is the expectation of the NWS-ish verb arādu here instead. Rainey apparently agrees with me on this last statement, even though he doesn't state so specifically. While yupi might seem a bit outside the box compared to the other two terms, it fits linguistically and it makes sense in both contexts. And what is more, it provides clear explanation for the gloss in 151:20, a-na mi-ru-ti "to the appearance," which reinforces yupi over and against both Eg. wpwt and NWS ʿbd.
