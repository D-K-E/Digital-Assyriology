(001) Rib-Haddi spoke t[o] the king of the lands: I [fell] at the feet [of my lord, the kin]g, seven times and seven times. Look, [from] way back, Byblos has been the loyal city [of] my [lo]rd, the sun god of the regions, and [l]ook, as for me, I am the footstool of the king, my lord, and his loyal servant.

(013) Furthermore, "Why does Rib-Haddi keep sending a tablet this way to the palace? He is more stressed out about Ṣumur than (even) his brothers!" Look, as for me, hostility has been against me for five years. Thus I keep writing to my lord. You see, I am neither li[ke] YapaʾHaddi nor l[ike Zi]mredda; the entire band of brothers has dese[rted]. There is hostility against me on account of Ṣumur. Here now its com[miss]ioner is dead and I am now sick. I was (there) in [Ṣumur when a]ll its people fl[ed. So may] my lord dispatch [a commissioner and troop]s with him as quick[ly as possible so that [he may] protect it, and I myself need not pro[tect it]; I split from the two of them who were [against me]. So how does the king say, "Why does Rib-Haddi keep send[ing] a tablet to the presence of his lord about wrongdoing comm[itted] in the past? Here now, is not the very same thing now being done to me?
