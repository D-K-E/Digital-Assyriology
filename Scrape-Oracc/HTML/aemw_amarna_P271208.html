(008) Look, I am a loyal servant of the king, the sun god. As for my speech, to the king I speak (only) truthful words. May the king, my lord, heed the words of his loyal servant. May the irpi-official take a stand in Ṣumur and bring Haʾip to you so that he may inspect him and find out what he has to say. If it pleases you, then appoint as its commissioner one respected by the city rulers of the ki[ng].

(025) May <my> lord heed [my] wor[ds]! Now ʾAziru, son of Abdi-Aširta, together with his brothers, is in Damascus! So send the regular army so that it may capture him and so that the land of the king may be pacified. If things stay as they are, then Ṣumur will not stand.

(034) Furthermore, may the king, my lord, heed the words of his loyal servant. There is no silver to pay for horses; everything to keep us alive is depleted! So give me 30 pairs of horses along with chariots. I have maryannu warriors, but I do not have horses in order to march against the king's foes. Thus I am afraid and thus I have not yet gone to Ṣumur.
