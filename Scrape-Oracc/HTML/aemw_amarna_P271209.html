(001) Speak [to Aziru], the ruler of Amurru, [the message] of the king, your lord, saying: The ruler of Byblos [has spoken] to you, (he) whom his brother cast out at the gate, [saying: T]ake me away and bring me into my city. (022) The king has now heard, saying: "You are on good terms with the ruler of Qedeš." You have been sharing food and beer together! So it is true! Why are you doing thus? Why are you on good terms with the man against whom the king is opposed? If you had acted out of loyalty, you would have seen (that) your intention(s) and his intention(s) are at odds. You no longer trust in the ideals you used to practice. What changed you in their midst so that you are not on the king's side?

(042) When you wrote, saying--"May the king, my lord, release me this year and I will c[ome] next year to the presence of the king, [my] lo[rd]; (if) that is impossible, my son [will co]me [to the king]"--the king, your lord, herewith releases you this year in accordance with what you have said. Come yourself or send your son, and you will see the king at whose sight all the lands live. But do not say, saying: "Release me this year, as well." If it is impossible to come before the king, your lord, send your son to the king, your lord, in your place. If it is impossible, let him come.
