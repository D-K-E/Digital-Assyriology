(006) Be[hold], the king, my lord, says: "Why do you constantly write me?" Look, as for me, there is no city ruler from Ṣumur who supports me. You see, everyone is against me. The two Egyptian men whom I sent to the palace have yet to come forth. Did I not write to the king: "There is no one who can bring my tablet to the palace!" These two men here will ostensibly deliver my tablet to the king. But <n>ow, they have not come forth! For this reason I am afraid and have turned to my lord.

(035) Furthermore, who are they, the(se) sons of Abdi-Aširta, the slave, the do[g, that they capt]ure for [themselves] the cities of the city rulers of the king? (You think) they are with you? Th[eir] cities [belong to] Aziru! The king cannot (so much as) ent[er] their cities. From Ṣ[umur up to] Ullaza, the city where he has be[en sending] chariots, for them there is no peace. I previously so[ught] to send a mess[enger wi]th troops and [chariots to] Ṣumur, but Azir[u kille]d [Pa]wura. As for troop<s>, I myself [sent] me[n] to Egypt and guardsmen were sent to me in their charge. Consequently, I sent this man.

(053) Moreover, did I not write to the king: "Now as for the two Egyptian men, may they themselves, my very breath, come forth to me." Yet they have not come. If the regular army does not emerge this year, a[ll] the regions will turn over to the apiru men. But if the heart of the king is not inclined to send the regular army, may he write to Yanhamu and Pihuru: "March along with your city rulers; capture Amurru." They will capture it in one day.

As for this man of mine, may the king send him as quickly as possible and may he provide guardsmen to guard his loyal servant, as well as the city and men of Meluhha with them, according to the custom of your forefathers.

(083) Furthermore, when the king says, "Guard! May you be on guard!" who will [protect me? You see, in] the days of [my] fathers, [the provisions of the king were at their disposal and] a [royal] defense unit was (stationed) [wi]th them and t[hey were at peace]. [But] as for me, the hos[tility agains]t me is [severe]. I myself [fe]ar my own working class! Therefore I kept wri[ting to] the palace for defense and men of Meluhha, but you [offered] not even one strong unit. What am I to do? May the king send guardsmen and men of Meluhha to protect me. May the city not ally with apiru.
