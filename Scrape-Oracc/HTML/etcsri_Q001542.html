(i 1) Ninĝišzida is the personal god of Gudea, ruler of Lagaš, who built the E-ana.

ii 1 1 d inana

(ii 1) For Inana, the lady of all lands, his lady, after she had looked at him with her life-giving look, Gudea, (whose) name is everlasting, ruler of Lagaš, the builder of Ninĝirsu's E-ninnu, being a ruler of Lagaš with broad wisdom and a slave who loves his lady, he made a drawing on the brick-mould's frame, and made a standard shine at the clay pit. He mixed the clay in a pure place, and made the (first) brick in an undefiled place. He purified the foundation pit by carrying around fire, and anointed the foundation pegs with oil of princely fragrance. He built her beloved temple, the E-ana, within Girsu for her.
