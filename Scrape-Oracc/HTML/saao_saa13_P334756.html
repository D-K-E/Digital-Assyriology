(r 4) But why do I s[peak] so much? This is the gist of the whole discourse. I may weep before all the lands, but there is no one who would give heart to me. So it is to you alone that I look — you who are my lord. It is before you that I have wept. If your gods [have m]oved you to have pity for me, then wipe away my tears. [I]f not, tell me, "Go and die!" [Then I wo]uld go away and die. What (else) could I say? I have entreated the crown prince for [...]. [......] offerings.
