(5) As of now, Fort Šama'unu is abandoned. There is nobody there except 200 scratch soldiers, and there is no food there except for the travel provisions which they carry with them. Moreover, they have cut off the water between us and the land of Rašu. Nobo[dy] from Rašu has (been able to) c[ome] to you.

(r 1) [Abi-y]aqar, the P[uq]udean, does not wish the recovery of the land in the king's presence, he is looking for revenge. He has changed the timetable which he had set. Obliging poverty and robbery, he is (thinking) to take booty for himself. He does not wish the clasping of hands.

(r 10) The captive Elamite held in Abi-yaqar's prison, who k[nows] my affairs and had heard (them) in the presence of Abi-yaqar and seen my letter — Abi-yaqar's palace manager released him for one mina of silver. And when he came, he testified for me before the legate of Rašu so we were not killed, but our sons are being held as hostages.
