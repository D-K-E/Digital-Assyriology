(11) (As to) the recruitment officer of whom the ki[ng, my lord, wrote]: "What is wrong that you have separated him [from] his brothers?" — is Ahu-ila'i not a servant of the king? If I keep him separate from his brothers, (it is because) he is (the most) reliable man among his brothers. [Inste]ad of seizing Ahu-ila'i for the guard duty, which is mutually fixed for our bread and water, (I swear) by the gods of the king, my lord, that ever since he came to Bit-Dakuri, I have not interfered in any way with Ahu-ila'i; and beyond that I say to the king, my lord: "He is a witness and a w[ild b]ull, ...[...]"
