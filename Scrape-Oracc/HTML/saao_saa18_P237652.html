(22) Ze[rutiya] broug[ht] in Bel-ipuš and Be[l]-bu[lliṭ] from Dilbat, (but) the king did not listen to what they said, and Ṣill[aya] has cunningly returned th[em] to Dilbat, telling them to mobilise archers. [I]f they have not mobilised archers, shouldn't he mobilise some, if there are none? He sends back men who have com[e] to him, telling himself: "They should not make my affairs known and no witnesses against me should stay here." May the king ask Zerutiya why he brought them in.
